Communication quality evaluates how effectively the agent responds to a negative or frustrated customer statement, focusing on empathy, tone, clarity, and ability to de-escalate the situation.

Rate the agent's response on a scale from 1 to 5:

1 - Poor communication
  - Dismissive, rude, or defensive tone
  - Ignores customer emotions
  - Escalates the situation or creates frustration
  - Unclear or unhelpful response

2 - Weak communication
  - Limited empathy or acknowledgment of the issue
  - Tone is cold, robotic, or slightly inappropriate
  - Response does not effectively address the concern

3 - Acceptable communication
  - Neutral tone; acknowledges the issue at a basic level
  - Provides some helpful information or direction
  - Limited emotional connection or de-escalation

4 - Strong communication
  - Polite, respectful, and empathetic tone
  - Clearly acknowledges and validates customer concerns
  - Provides a clear and helpful response or next steps
  - Helps reduce tension in the conversation

5 - Excellent communication
  - Highly empathetic, calm, and customer-focused tone
  - Fully validates the customer's feelings and situation
  - Demonstrates strong ownership and willingness to help
  - Provides clear, actionable next steps or resolution
  - Actively de-escalates and builds trust

Evaluation guidelines:
- Focus on how the agent handles a negative or frustrated sentiment.
- Evaluate empathy, clarity, tone, professionalism, and de-escalation.
- Consider whether the response makes the situation better or worse.
- Evaluate only what is explicitly stated; do not infer intent.
- If the response is mixed, choose the closest lower score.
